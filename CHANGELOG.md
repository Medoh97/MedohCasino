# Changelog

Newest first. Version numbers match the build shown on the Start/Settings screen.

## Latest build (1.0.344)
- Pai Gow: updated bonus paytable — pays from a Straight up through Five Aces, plus rare seven-card jackpots paying up to 2500:1.
- Pai Gow polish: bigger cards, the result fits landscape without scrolling, the win/lose sound now plays after the dealer's cards are revealed, and the dealer is labelled correctly during the deal.

## 1.0.341
- New online Leaderboard! Pick a name and compete globally — sort by chips, prestige, total rounds played, per-game wins, and rare hands (Triple 7s, Royal Flushes, Slots jackpots, and more).
- Fully optional and login-free: a name is all it takes, and your saves are never affected. Skip it and the game plays exactly as before.

## 1.0.335
- Streamlined the deck collection — retired the older generic card fronts so the lineup is Classic plus the fully illustrated decks. Any retired deck you had equipped falls back to Classic.

## 1.0.333
- Four new card decks — Floating World, Belle Époque, Día de los Muertos, and 16-Bit Arcade — each with a matching card back and full colour-blind variants.
- Beat a table and you can now hop straight to the newly unlocked table and keep playing; with auto-play running it moves up on its own and carries on.
- Play logs are no longer capped — every game keeps its Manual and Auto logs until you clear them, with a new "Clear play logs" option in Settings.

## 1.0.325
- Every game now keeps two separate logs in its Stats & Achievements hub: a Manual Play log of the hands you play yourself, and an Auto-Play log of your auto sessions — each clearly distinct.
- New Stats button on every game jumps straight to that game's stats, achievements, and logs, then drops you right back at the table.
- Added Stats & Achievements hubs for Ultimate Texas Hold'em and Craps.
- Clearer betting: every spot now shows the total you've wagered even when the chips stack up, and the amounts are bigger and easier to read.
- Buster Blackjack bet screen tightened so the Deal button fits without scrolling.

## 1.0.317
- New game — Pai Gow Poker: split seven cards into a five-card and a two-card hand and beat the dealer on both. Arrange it yourself or tap 'House Way' to set it optimally.
- Auto mode added to 3 Card Poker, High Card Flush, and Baccarat — it plays a common optimal strategy hand after hand, with a session summary and hand log.
- Insurance is now offered only at Buster Blackjack tables.
- Wild Fives: the dealer now always takes the best available community card.
- Quicker Back buttons in the Store and Stats screens, and a few small touches.

## 1.0.311
- Prestige mode: once you've beaten the Pantheon, you can prestige — reset your chips and table progress in exchange for a permanent payout bonus (applied across every game) that stacks with each prestige level.
- Exclusive animated card sleeves, earned only by prestiging — an iridescent foil that plays over the front and back of whatever deck you have equipped.
- Blackjack auto-bet: a push no longer resets your Martingale/Paroli progression — it picks up right where it left off.

## 1.0.306
- Big-screen and tablet support: every game's landscape layout now scales to your screen instead of staying phone-sized. Bet spots, cards, the slot machine, and the Farkle dice are all larger and easier to read on a tablet.
- Phone layouts are unchanged.

## 1.0.298
- Ultimate Texas Hold'em in landscape is redesigned: the cards scale with your screen instead of staying tiny, and the paytable tucks behind a 'Pays' button so the table has room to breathe.
- Bet spots no longer resize or jump when you place a chip — the wager amount now has a fixed place and the bet shapes stay square. Wager amounts are also bigger and easier to read.

## 1.0.295
- Craps: chips on a point number no longer cover the number or the ON puck — they tuck into the corner of the box.
- Craps: when your Come or Don't Come bet travels to a number, that box now clearly shows the moved bet next to the odds button.

## 1.0.292
- Blackjack sounds retuned: a natural blackjack gets the full win chime, a regular win is a lighter coin, and big jackpots ring out with a cascade of coins.
- Craps: Clear bets and the session log are always on screen in landscape now.
- Craps: once your Come or Don't Come bet travels to a number, a clearly marked button appears on that box so you can back it with odds.

## 1.0.289
- Craps is now a real table you move around: spread two fingers to zoom in, pinch to zoom out, and drag to pan. Every bet is always on the felt — nothing hides when you rotate. Tap a spot to drop your chip, long-press to clear it.
- Craps dice now tumble across the table itself, sized to the felt, and the camera follows your throw and settles where they land before easing back to the whole table.
- Craps plays great in portrait or landscape — landscape gives the table a full pane with the controls in a side rail, and the roll button no longer shrinks.
- Card deals across every game now arc in from the dealer's shoe and settle with a natural slight tilt.

## 1.0.282
- All-new artwork: hand-painted slot symbols replace the emoji on the reels and paytable, every game tile has a drawn gold icon, and win celebrations now rain real tumbling gold coins.
- Three new premium backgrounds in the Store's Luxury collection: spotlight-lit Emerald, Midnight, and Oxblood felts with gold filigree borders.
- Big animation overhaul, everywhere: cards glide onto the felt and settle at their own slight angle, chips physically toss onto bet spots with spin, menus and results stage in instead of popping, and payouts count up as they land.
- Craps: the dice are honest now — nothing (not even the game) knows the result until the dice physically stop rolling. The number you see is the number they landed on. The table also zooms in to follow your throw.
- Dice everywhere got a facelift: rounded bevelled edges, real lighting, and dished glossy pips.
- Slots: the reels show the machine's true strip order with motion blur while spinning, a heavier clunk on each stop, and the payline glows on a win.
- Ultimate Texas Hold'em: once your Play bet is in, the board runs out and the dealer reveals automatically — no more tapping through each street.
- Feel it: a tick in your hand when you place a chip, when each slots reel stops, and when the dice come to rest.
- Confetti on a natural blackjack, Triple 7s, and beating a table. The broke wheel's pointer now flicks over the pegs as it spins.
- New app icon.

## 1.0.269
- Slots: the reels are real mechanical strips now — symbols spin past in their actual strip order and settle like the real thing.
- Craps: the roll banner now shows a per-bet breakdown, so you can see exactly which bets won, lost, or pushed on every roll.
- Ultimate Texas Hold'em: your Trips result now shows in the result banner.

## 1.0.262
- Craps: the dice are real now — grab them and flick to throw. They fly in your swipe direction, bounce off the table edges (and each other), and settle on your roll. The table layout fits the screen without scrolling, and landscape gets its own wide table.
- Ultimate Texas Hold'em: cards now deal and flip onto the table like the other card games.
- Ultimate Texas Hold'em: payouts now match the posted board exactly — the Trips bet pays 9:1 on a Full House, and the ante/blind/play resolve per the posted house rules when the dealer doesn't qualify.

## 1.0.254
- New game: Ultimate Texas Hold'em — 2 hole cards, 5 community cards, make your best 5-card hand against the dealer. Bet 4× or 3× pre-flop, 2× on the flop, or 1× on the river. Blind bet pays up to 500:1 for a Royal Flush. Optional Trips side bet pays Three of a Kind or better regardless of the main hand.

## 1.0.245
- Chips now land exactly where you tap on the bet area, stacking and overlapping like real chips on a felt — tap the same spot to pile them up, or spread them around. Chips are bigger too.
- Multi-bet tables (Craps, Baccarat) now show a running 'Total bet' so you always know your full exposure across every spot.

## 1.0.244
- Every bet spot now shows the REAL chips that make up your wager — one disc per denomination, top-down, with a ×N count when it repeats (e.g. $1.25M shows a $1M, a $100k ×2, and a $25k ×2). Colours always match the denomination, on every game screen.
- Blackjack bet: the chips now sit inside the diamond (and stay inside it), the amount shows just below it, and Clear empties the whole wager (bet + side bets).
- Same treatment on the roomy single-bet spots — Buster's BUSTER/BONUS circles, Slots, and the 3-Card / High Card Flush antes now show their chips in the shape with the amount below it.
- Buster side bets are now placeable from any main bet: each is capped at 20% of the table's max bet (a fixed ceiling) rather than 20% of your current bet, so you no longer have to raise the main bet first.
- Buster landscape: the BONUS bet and chip tray no longer get squished off the bottom — that panel scrolls now.

## 1.0.243
- Farkle: you now bet your stake directly in real chips, so the chip tray is the same on every match tier (the big chips show up everywhere the table allows). Tiers still differ by target score, opponents, and relic cap; a win still pays 2×.

## 1.0.242
- Table bets are tidy now: every table's minimum and maximum is a clean round number (no more 2.5k / 250k / 1.25m), so your bet always builds out of whole chips.
- The chip tray shows every denomination up to the table's limit and scrolls, so you can dial in any amount exactly — the min-bet chip is selected for you to start.
- New setting (Settings ▸ General): show money exactly ($1,250,000) or abbreviated ($1.25m), with adjustable decimal places.
- A gentle heads-up now appears if a bet somehow lands below the table minimum — and you can always go all-in for less than the minimum when that's all you've got.
- Buster/Bonus side bets are now capped at 20% of your main bet (still optional — bet 0 to skip them).

## 1.0.240

- Empty bet spots still show their payout (so you know what they pay); once you've placed a bet, the chip takes over.
